About me

I am currently pursuing a PhD degree in Electrical Engineering, King Abdullah University of Science and Technology. I am honored to be part of Sensors group led by Professor Khaled Salama, as well as Communication and Computing Systems (CCSL) group led by Professor Ahmed Eltawil. My research interests cover a variety of interdisciplinary research topics, ranging from material growth and characterization, light-emitting diodes and laser design, 2D materials based gas sensors to digital system design, and neuromorphic computing.

Research interests 

Neuromorphic computing  Spiking neural network  Reconfigurable digital design

My current research interest lies in the hardware-and-software codesign of an energy-efficient reconfigurable hardware system design targetting artificial intelligence based applications. On the software side, I am working on optimizing spiking neural works for fast and energy-efficient information processing. On the hardware side, I am developing a digital neuromorphic hardware system with a reconfigurable architecture and energy-efficient event communication scheme.
